# Product Context

## Register

product

## Product Purpose

This repository supports an MSc dissertation project on training-free, evidence-grounded extraction of seizure-frequency information from epilepsy clinic letters. The interface work exists to make project state, evaluation evidence, decisions, artifacts, and next actions legible to the researcher and supervisor.

## Primary Users

- The dissertation author, reviewing progress across experiments and writing sessions.
- A supervisor or reviewer, checking whether claims are evidence-backed and whether uncertainty is explicit.
- Future maintainers, reconstructing why a harness, provider, or visibility decision was made.

## Core UX Promise

The product should answer four questions quickly:

- What is currently true?
- What evidence supports it?
- What remains uncertain?
- What should happen next?

## Tone

Research-grade, calm, exact, source-backed, and editorial. The interface may feel distinctive, but it must never obscure evidence, uncertainty, or governance boundaries.

## Anti-References

- Generic SaaS dashboards with repeated statistic cards.
- Dark cyberpunk AI control rooms.
- Healthcare stock-photo interfaces.
- Decorative motion that delays access to project state.
- Marketing landing pages that turn evidence into slogans.

## Strategic Principles

- Product UI serves the research task.
- Every important claim should expose source evidence.
- Real clinical-data governance boundaries must stay visible.
- Distinctive design is welcome when it increases trust, scanability, and memory.
- Dense information is acceptable when hierarchy remains clear.
